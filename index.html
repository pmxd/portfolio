Sep 26, 2014 + My Perspective On Interactive Design

This entry is about some of my thoughts when it comes to interactive design.

I recently chose a group topic to study and write about for my module Directed Study 1 CS4042. The topic that my group and I chose was “Designing Interactive Installations For Home, Health and Play”. When choosing our topic I had suggested to my fellow group members, Eddie and Matus, that we select this topic as it appealed to me the most and I thought it would be an interesting assignment to undertake together. As it turns out I was right. The vast amount of information available on the internet was quite overwhelming at first. We had originally 4 members in our group but this soon dropped to three because of lack of attendance.

The first step in this project was to divide the workload evenly. I chose to take the health topic and my colleges chose home and play. From here I found a project that Volkswagen had undertaken titled “the fun theory”. I fount their approached to Interactive media interesting and thought their use of technology to improve human behavior was an exciting field to study.

I then educated myself further on Interactive Installations and how they could help make the world a healthier place. Through my findings I realized that if more interactive installations were set in place throughout the first world we could live in a happier, less stressful and healthier world. By simply lining subway stairs with a white and black sheet and making the steps play notes as you walked over them we could encourage 66% more people to actually use the stairs rather than an escalator.

All in all, I believe that the world could greatly benefit from integrating technology with everyday tasks. The possibilities for interactive design are endless and it is certainly a field in which I will be researching further as I advance through my college life.

- IX Design

Aug 13, 2014 + Reflection on Group Work

The group topic for Directed Study 1 in CS4042 that my group and I chose is “Designing Interactive Installations For Home, Health and Play”. My colleagues for this project were Matus and Eddie. The first task for the group work was to divide the work load and select our chosen sub topics. The topic I chose was Health. My workmates then chose home and play. I then suggested to my group that we use a program named “dropbox”. This program made it easy to transfer files and communicate more efficiently. Although this program was extremely helpful, it also had its faults. We had encountered some problems while two people worked on the same file. This would create separate files and one person would have to go back and alter one of the files.

Before we began to research our selected topics I decided to draw up a skeleton file and place it in dropbox. This gave some similar structure between our individual work and would make it easier to put our work together when we had finished.

From here we branched out and did some research on our individual topics. We met as a group regularly to discuss our topics and share information. For the presentation Eddie took the lead. He set a format for the slides and uploaded his slides to dropbox. From here, Matus and I added our slides edited the document until we were all happy with the final document.

I thought that our presentation went well. We decided not to focus on cramming information onto the slides but instead used a few bullet points and some pictures to illustrate our thoughts. I was also interested to see our different approaches to how we presented our information. My workmates wrote out everything they had planned to say and made flash cards with the information. My approach was a bit more laid back as I just made flash cards and didn’t have a specific script to stick to. I believe that I present better when I do not have my thoughts scripted and I think the presentation feels more organic.

All in all I really enjoyed this project and it is something that I will be researching further. I do not believe there was a specific leader in our group as we took turns with our tasks and functioned as a team very well.

Thanks for reading,

Paul
